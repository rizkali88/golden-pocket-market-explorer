# Live Pilot: US Health Care REITs

Date prepared: 2026-05-11

## Pilot Focus

Industry selected: `US Health Care REITs`, with a particular emphasis on senior-housing-heavy operators.

Reason for selection:

- real estate has been one of the stronger improving sectors in the starter model
- health care REIT fundamentals are being supported by occupancy recovery and aging-population demand
- the industry is showing earnings momentum even though long rates remain relatively elevated

This makes it a useful pilot for a 3-6 month spot-trading framework.

## Market Context

- The 10-year Treasury yield was `4.43%` on `2026-05-05`, according to FRED.
- The Real Estate Select Sector SPDR ETF (`XLRE`) closed at `$44.74` on `2026-05-06`, according to State Street.
- On the same `2026-05-06` State Street snapshot, `XLRE` showed:
  - `30-day SEC yield`: `3.19%`
  - `fund distribution yield`: `3.13%`
  - `price/cash flow`: `18.85`
  - `price/earnings`: `34.16`
  - `price/earnings ratio FY1`: `37.33`

Interpretation:

- real estate is working despite a still-firm rate backdrop
- that reduces the odds this is only a short-lived "lower yields" bounce
- it also means stock selection matters, because valuation is no longer outright cheap across the whole group

## Industry Structure

State Street's `XLRE` holdings data dated `2026-05-06` shows:

- `Health Care REITs` are `16.66%` of the fund
- top sector holdings include `Welltower`, `Ventas`, and other large real estate platforms

This is useful because the health care REIT sleeve is large enough to matter, but still specific enough to evaluate as a distinct industry theme.

## Fundamental Read

### Welltower (`WELL`)

Based on Welltower's first-quarter 2026 business update dated `2026-04-28`:

- normalized FFO per diluted share was `$1.47`, up `23%` year over year
- full-year FFO guidance midpoint was raised to `$6.28`
- same-store senior housing operating portfolio NOI increased `22.1%`
- same-store revenue growth was `9.5%`
- occupancy gains contributed `370 bps`
- same-store NOI margin expanded `320 bps`

Interpretation:

- this is strong proof that senior-housing demand is converting into cash flow
- the operating leverage is real, not just a valuation rerating

### Ventas (`VTR`)

Based on Ventas's first-quarter 2026 results released `2026-04-27`:

- normalized FFO per share was `$0.94`, up `9%` year over year
- total company NOI grew `14%`
- total company same-store cash NOI grew `9%`
- SHOP same-store cash NOI grew `more than 15%`
- U.S. SHOP average occupancy grew `370 bps`
- full-year normalized FFO midpoint was raised from `$3.83` to `$3.86`
- net debt to further adjusted EBITDA improved to `5.0x`
- liquidity stood at `$5.5 billion`

Interpretation:

- Ventas is showing the same broad demand pattern as Welltower
- the guidance raise matters because it confirms management sees durability, not a one-quarter blip

### Healthpeak (`DOC`)

Based on Healthpeak's first-quarter 2026 results released `2026-05-05`:

- net income was `$0.28` per share
- FFO as adjusted was `$0.45` per share
- senior housing same-store adjusted NOI grew `13.8%`
- Janus Living IPO proceeds were approximately `$880 million`
- Healthpeak raised 2026 earnings guidance
- full-year total same-store cash adjusted NOI guidance was reaffirmed at `-1.0%` to `1.0%`

Interpretation:

- this is a more mixed setup than `WELL` or `VTR`
- there is senior-housing upside, but the broader portfolio is not as cleanly accelerating

## Pilot Verdict

Rating: `Constructive, but buy-on-pullback`

Why this industry qualifies for a pilot:

- operating data is improving
- guidance is being raised
- demographic demand is favorable
- balance sheets are not flashing the same kind of stress seen in weaker real estate segments
- the group is rallying even without a major drop in the 10-year yield

Why this is not a perfect "undervalued" pocket:

- leaders are already near highs
- broad REIT valuation is not especially cheap on simple headline multiples
- rate sensitivity remains a real risk if Treasury yields push materially higher

## Trading Framework For A 3-6 Month Pilot

Vehicle options:

- `XLRE` for diversified exposure
- `WELL` for the strongest operating trend
- `VTR` for a slightly more balanced risk/reward profile

Preferred approach:

- `Tier 1`: start with `XLRE` as the pilot vehicle
- `Tier 2`: add `VTR` or `WELL` only on pullbacks or after confirmed support holds

Execution logic:

- avoid chasing a sharp breakout day
- prefer entries after 3-7 day consolidations or pullbacks into support
- size smaller if the 10-year yield is rising at the same time

Invalidation logic:

- thesis weakens if long yields rise sharply and REITs stop outperforming
- thesis weakens if occupancy momentum fades in the next earnings cycle
- thesis weakens if `XLRE` loses its recent breakout zone and cannot reclaim it

## Bottom Line

If we run only one live pilot right now, `US Health Care REITs` are a reasonable choice.

They are not the cheapest industry in the market, but they do offer something more valuable for a 3-6 month trade:

- improving fundamentals
- visible catalysts
- clear institutional leadership
- a simple ETF proxy

The cleanest expression is: `constructive industry, but wait for disciplined entries instead of chasing`.
